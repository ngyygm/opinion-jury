## 1. Verdict Summary

**Behavioral classification: CLASS CONSUMPTION (阶层消费)**

Confidence: HIGH | Consensus: NEAR-UNANIMOUS

After six rounds of multi-blind deliberation, all six anonymous panelists converged on the conclusion that Tim's use of selective information at the matchmaking corner objectively constituted class-based consumption. This is a behavioral assessment, not a personal condemnation.

## 2. Key Findings

### 2.1 What happened

Tim (潘天鸿), founder of 影视飓风 (16.3M B站 followers), visited a public matchmaking corner with a resume containing selectively weakened information: "domestic middle school education" (actual: University of Kent, UK), "courier-related" father (actual: Yuantong Express president), "photographer/video production" occupation (actual: CEO of a company with 100M+ yuan annual revenue). The segment lasted approximately 50 seconds and constituted about 5% of a fan Q&A video.

### 2.2 Core behavioral assessment

The panel identified a critical **directional asymmetry** between ordinary matchmaking participants who upwardly package their profiles (driven by insecurity) and Tim's downward disguise (designed to manufacture contrast). This asymmetry is the ethical core that distinguishes "creative content" from "class consumption."

- **Upward packaging** (ordinary people): "I do management work" (actually warehouse manager) — motivated by self-consciousness
- **Downward disguise** (Tim): "Domestic middle school education" (actually UK university graduate) — motivated by contrast creation

### 2.3 The consent gap

The panel unanimously found that "notifying people that filming is occurring" is not equivalent to "informing people that they may become the punchline of a viral video seen by millions." The informed consent provided was inadequate by any professional standard — even kindergartens require signed consent forms for using children's photos.

### 2.4 Dual responsibility

The panel concluded that both parties bear responsibility:

1. **Tim's original behavior** was the source — he chose to use selective information, chose to film in a public matchmaking venue, and as a top-tier creator should have anticipated the consequences.
2. **Marketing accounts' decontextualized clip propagation** was the key amplifier — they extracted 50 seconds from a larger video, added sensational titles, mixed in footage from other sources, and weaponized the content for clicks.

Both should be addressed; neither exonerates the other.

## 4. Institutional Recommendations

The panel produced a comprehensive three-stage institutional framework:

### 4.1 Front-end: Informed Consent Norms
- **Inform:** Clearly tell subjects about the platform, expected viewership, and content purpose
- **Consent:** Explicit opt-in, not default consent
- **Right to withdraw:** Subjects can request deletion or blurring post-publication
- **Platform enforcement:** Require creators to label informed consent status; reduce recommendation weight for non-compliant content (proposed by SEAT-1)
- **Standard template:** Platform-provided informed consent template to minimize compliance burden (proposed by SEAT-3)

### 4.2 Middle-end: Creator Responsibility
- Heightened class sensitivity for top-tier creators
- Industry self-regulation using this incident as a case study
- Topic sensitivity checklists for public-space filming

### 4.3 Back-end: Secondary Propagation Rules (proposed by SEAT-5)
- **Source attribution:** Clips must link to original full video
- **Proportion labeling:** Clips under 5% of original must disclose percentage
- **Title truthfulness:** Clip titles cannot contain information absent from original video

## 7. Boundary Conditions

This verdict operates under the following conditions:

1. **Behavioral, not personal.** This is an assessment of a specific action, not a judgment of Tim's character or his broader body of work.
2. **Correction space exists.** Behavioral critique and personal growth are not contradictory. A formal apology and genuine process improvement should be given real credit.
3. **Institutional focus.** The most valuable outcome of this deliberation is not the classification of one incident, but the institutional framework that could prevent similar incidents across the entire content industry.
4. **Dual responsibility.** Addressing only the creator without addressing the marketing account ecosystem would leave the structural problem unresolved.
