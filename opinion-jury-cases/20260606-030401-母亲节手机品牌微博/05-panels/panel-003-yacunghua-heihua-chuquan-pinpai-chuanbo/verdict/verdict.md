# Blind Adjudication Verdict: Panel-003

## Issue: 亚文化黑话出圈：品牌用'同人小甜文''好大儿'等饭圈术语面向公众的传播合理性

**Adjudication Type:** Blind (adjudicator has no knowledge of actor identities or private data)
**Rounds Reviewed:** 6 (36 total turns)
**Verdict Date:** 2026-06-06

---

## 1. Overall Judgment

**Brand communications using subcultural fandom terminology for mass public dissemination, without audience segmentation testing or risk control review, constitute an unreasonable communication strategy failure.** The reasonability of such communications is not absolute but relative to the communication scenario, target audience, and risk control processes in place.

Using subcultural terminology in precisely matched scenarios (e.g., targeting young users on Bilibili) can be reasonable. Using niche terminology in full-spectrum mass public communications without any risk controls is an unacceptable execution failure.

**Confidence: HIGH | Consensus Level: STRONG**

---

## 2. Balanced Assessment

### 2.1 Arguments AGAINST Reasonability (Strength: STRONG)

The arguments against the brand's communication reasonability gained overwhelming traction across six rounds:

1. **Audience Barrier is Objective Fact**: The terminology used by the brand covers a demographic far narrower than what mass communication requires. SEAT-4 (a 29-year-old delivery driver) and his 55-year-old rural mother could not understand the campaign text -- direct empirical evidence that the audience barrier is real, not theoretical.

2. **Contextual Misalignment**: Mother's Day scenarios demand warm, inclusive narratives. Fandom-coded expressions clash with this context. Even users who could decode the terms might find them inappropriate for the occasion.

3. **Value Controversy Amplification**: The "two husbands" image specifically triggered deep controversy about marital respect and gender power dynamics. This transformed a communication efficiency failure into a full-blown brand crisis (two public apologies, Wuhan University statement, China Women's Daily criticism).

4. **Absence of Basic Risk Controls**: No audience readability testing, no scenario assessment, no sensitivity review. The brand skipped every standard risk control step that could have prevented the failure.

5. **Verified Evidence**: CLAIM_GATE sources confirm institutional criticism (Wuhan University stated "strongly disagree"; China Women's Daily stated "don't treat offense as creativity") and two brand apologies.

### 2.2 Arguments FOR Reasonability (Strength: MODERATE)

Legitimate points in the brand's favor were raised but always required qualification:

1. **Creative Direction Had Social Value**: Challenging the stereotype that mothers must only be "gentle, hardworking, selfless" is a worthwhile goal. The creative direction of "showing mothers' diverse sides" has merit.

2. **UGC Activity Form Was Sound**: Inviting users to submit their own stories about their mothers is a reasonable engagement mechanism. The form itself was not the problem.

3. **Scenario-Relative Reasonability**: In precisely targeted scenarios (e.g., Bilibili's 18-25 user base), the same terminology might be effective. SEAT-2 confirmed that the same copy would likely not have failed as severely on Bilibili.

4. **Cultural Materials Were Not Inherently Problematic**: Star-chasing, fan fiction writing, and pet ownership are legitimate aspects of mothers' lives. The problem was the presentation frame and communication scenario, not the cultural content itself.

**Key Qualification**: All arguments for reasonability required the caveat "in the right scenario." The brand's failure was not in wanting to innovate, but in deploying niche-targeted content through mass broadcast channels without any risk controls.

## 5. Real-World Risk Assessment

**Risk Level: MODERATE**

The brand's actual public opinion risk has already materialized: two public apologies, criticism from Wuhan University and China Women's Daily, sustained trending discussion. However, the risk is primarily confined to brand reputation rather than product sales.

**Why risk is limited:**
- Product (smartphone) quality is unaffected by marketing activity
- Product-oriented consumers distinguish between marketing and product quality
- Similar brand failures in the industry typically fade from public attention within weeks
- The discussion's consensus points to actionable improvements rather than brand boycotts

**Long-term assessment:** If the brand learns from this failure and improves its communication risk control processes, the negative impact can convert into a positive learning case. The discussion produced a concrete, actionable framework (four-dimension risk assessment + three-step mitigation + one guiding principle) that any brand can adopt.

## 6. Key Consensus Framework

The discussion produced a reusable analytical tool for evaluating subcultural terminology in public communications:

### Four-Dimension Risk Assessment Framework:
1. **Information encoding compatibility** -- Can the audience decode the message?
2. **Contextual fit** -- Does the message match the communication scenario?
3. **Content value sensitivity** -- Does the message carry controversial value presuppositions?
4. **Communication scenario matching** -- Is it precision-targeted or mass broadcast?

### Three-Step Risk Control Process:
1. Pre-release audience readability testing (at least 5 users across age segments)
2. Communication scenario matching assessment
3. Content value sensitivity review (gender, class, ethnicity, etc.)

### One Guiding Principle:
Brands should not speak on behalf of their target users. They should build platforms for users to express themselves in their own words.
